My name is Julia Klayman, and I am a student at George W. Hewlett High School. I have always had a passion to advocate for those who can not advocate for themselves, specifically children. I am the president of the Friendship Circle Organization, where I volunteer weekly with children with special needs. My love for research has also led me to participate in the National History Day Competition yearly, where I have made it to the national competition three times. I am also enrolled in the science research course at my school, where I have been researching projects related to UV light since sophomore year, as well as the AP Research Course, where I researched how occupations that prioritize the employment of veterans can exacerbate workforce-induced PTSD triggers. By taking a summer course through the University of Texas at Austin called Law and Society, I have learned that my interest in law will give me an opportunity to combine the three things that I am pasionate about the most: children, history, and research.

Research

I have been taking science research since 9th grade and it has always been one of my favorite classes. In both my freshamn and sophomore year, I worked on a project about UV light, aiming to identify and characterize the photoreceptor, UVR8, in a plant called Ceratopteris richardii. Building off the results from this project, in my junior year, I worked on a project determining the cytotoxic effect of Titanium Dioxide particle size and UV light on a different plant, Lemna minor. This course has sparked my interest in research, and I know that in the future I would love to continue working on these projects. During my junior year, I also enrolled in a course called AP Research. Throughout this course, I studied whether occupations that prioritize the employment of veterans can exacerbate workforce-induced triggers of PTSD. Taking this course allowed me to study a topic that I was truly passionate about, and learn the specifics regarding scholarly research.

Makeup

I have always loved makeup. I watched my mother when I was younger, and fell in love with the art of makeup. I began to watch Youtube videos and learned special techniques to get “the perfect look.” I started off with neutral tones, and as I got better, I moved on to brighter colors. When I get stressed, doing makeup is my creative outlet. Now, I do all of my friends and family’s makeup for any occasion, makeup for proms and other events, and makeup on myself just for fun. Learning about makeup has also inspired my interests regarding gender roles and the feminine beauty ideal. Makeup is an art for any person, regardless of their gender identity, and women specificially should not be held to such high standards, where they feel the need to look "beautiful" and "presentable" each day for others.

BBYO

The B'nai Brith Youth Organization (BBYO) is a non-profit organization that helps teach teens about their Jewish identity and provides incredible leadership programs. When I joined BBYO as a general member in 9th grade, I didn’t know anyone in the club. I ended up falling in love with BBYO and the people I met at meetings and conventions. I became more and more involved and in 10th grade, I ran for treasurer and won. During my junior year, I was the vice president on board, where I planned each meeting and wrote programs to ensure meetings would run smoothly. Currently, I am the president of my local BBYO chapter, Sababa. As president, I get to help all of the other board members with their duties such as recruitment and retainment, as well as ensuring that each member is having a great time at meetings and conventions.

National History Day

In 8th grade, the National History Day Fair was a requirement to take advanced social studies courses in the high school. In 9th grade, unlike any other group, I continued with history fair and it has become one of my favorite projects that I do each and every year. With great success in 8th grade, making it to the national competition, I was confident that my group and I would make it to nationals again. In 10th grade, we won second place at the county and the state level, moving onto nationals. It was an amazing experience to meet the other local and international students, and to present our hard work to the judges! My junior year, I completed a project with my partner titled: "Lucretia Mott and Elizabeth Cady Stanton: Leading the Barrier Breaking Movement Toward Women’s Equality." After placing second in the state competion, we made it to nationals. Although the competition was online, we still had an amazing experience by learning about history through others students topics, and learning an even more amount about our own.

Friendship Circle

In middle school, I was involved in Bulldog Buddies, a club where you spend time with students with special needs before and after school. When I was about to start high school, I knew I wanted to get more involved with other students with special needs because I enjoyed this club so much. I found the local chapter of the Friendship Circle Organization, and I knew it was the perfect volunteer opportunity for me. I was matched with a family and weekly I continue to go to their house to do homework, play games, practice communicating with others, and make sweet treats with both of the children. I work with both the son who has special needs and the daughter who does not. I love working with them, so I became more involved in the organization. I help with the summer and winter camps each year held for any child with special needs, where we take field trips to engage the children in activities that they wouldn’t normally get to do. I have been the student president since 9th grade, and help match volunteers with special friends just like mine. This organization is so special to me, as it allows me to both gain and provide true friendship to students with special needs.

Thailand

In the summer of 2019, I took a community service trip to Thailand. When I was there, I learned about Southeast Asian culture and took part in service projects. My favorite project was going to schools that did not gain enough government funding and helped teach the students English. We were able to teach them how to tell the time in English, how to say certain animals names and noises, as well as common phrases. While we were there, they also taught us some Thai. It was an incredible experience. We also volunteered at an elephant sanctuary. We learned how to clean, bathe, and care for the elephants, and learn about their journey to be released back into the wild.
